Safe leads and lead changes in competitive team sports.
Aaron Clauset, Marina Kogan and Sid Redner
Physical Review E 91, 062815 (2015).

During the post-acceptance production of this article, the editorial staff at Physical Review removed the following statement of ours from the acknowledgements of the paper:

By mutual agreement, author order was determined randomly, according to when the maximum lead size occurred during the Denver Nuggetsâ€“Milwaukee Bucks NBA game on 3 March 2015.

The reason given was vague and perfunctory:

Phys. Rev. does not print such information.

We asked that this information then be moved to an endnote, since we felt that it provided crucial clarifying information about the meaning of the author order as well as a cute practical demonstration of our results. Physical Review then responded with the following bizarre denial:

We have considered your request to restore the information about author order in this paper.

While we do allow statements to the effect that certain authors contributed equally, etc., we can not publish the statement that author order was determined by a specific random process. We recognize this statement adds color to the presentation and may make the paper more interesting to read. Nevertheless, this statement can be construed as one that discounts the importance of authorship or author order. We view such a potential message to the reader as contrary to the spirit of our editorial procedures and policies.

We hope that you understand our position.

Hence this page exists to correct the record and to clarify what exactly the author order means in the paper. Also, Physical Review, what the hell?
